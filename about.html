About

"Discover Langkawi: The Jewel of Kedah"

Pulau Langkawi, an archipelago consisting of 99 islands, is located in the Andaman Sea off the coast of northwestern Malaysia. Historically, Langkawi has been inhabited for thousands of years, with early settlers including indigenous tribes and later traders from China, India, and the Middle East, attracted by its strategic location along the maritime Silk Road. In the 15th century, Langkawi became part of the Sultanate of Kedah, and over the centuries, it witnessed various periods of colonization and conflict, including attacks by the Siamese and the British.

Mahsuri

A significant legend in Langkawi's history involves a woman named Mahsuri, who was falsely accused of adultery and cursed the island for seven generations before her execution. Langkawi remained relatively underdeveloped until the 1980s, when the Malaysian government initiated efforts to transform it into a major tourist destination, culminating in its designation as a duty-free island in 1987, which spurred significant economic growth and development.

Story Behind the Eagle

Pulau Langkawi is renowned for its rich cultural heritage closely tied to the majestic eagle. The island's name is believed to derive from the Malay words for "reddish-brown eagle", and eagles hold deep symbolic significance in Langkawi's folklore and traditions. Visitors can witness the thrill of eagle feeding sessions on Singa Besar Island, where these magnificent birds of prey swoop down to catch their prey from the waters. The iconic Langkawi Sky Bridge, perched 660 meters above the rainforest, offers breathtaking views of eagles soaring over the island's lush landscapes. Beyond these iconic attractions, Langkawi is home to diverse eagle species like the White-bellied Sea Eagle and Brahminy Kite, which can be spotted throughout the island, making the eagle a central figure in Langkawi's natural and cultural heritage.
