Langkawi

Culture & Heritage

Langkawi has a long history closely associated with the Kedah Sultanate. The island's name is thought to have existed since the 15th century and may derive from the Malay words for "reddish-brown eagle" or be related to the ancient name "Lanka" found in Indian literature.

Mahsuri's Tomb and Legacy

Mahsuri was a legendary figure in Langkawi's history. She was a young woman unjustly accused of adultery and executed, cursing Langkawi with 7 generations of bad luck before her death. Her tomb is a major tourist attraction, with a museum displaying artifacts from that time.

Traditional Villages and Crafts

Langkawi is known for its traditional villages and artisanal crafts. The Atma Alam Batik Art Village showcases the fine art of batik-making, while the Langkawi Craft Complex features demonstrations of local handicrafts like glass blowing, weaving, and carving

Religious & Cultural Sites

Langkawi is home to important religious and cultural sites like the Al-Hana Mosque, one of the largest mosques on the island. The Langkawi Cultural and Craft Complex also houses exhibits on the culture and heritage of Malaysia's 13 states.
